Founders

Chairman

R K Mehta
R.K. Mehta, a visionary entrepreneur with a deep-rooted concern for environmental sustainability, is the driving force behind the Progressive Biochar Society. Recognizing the devastating impact of deforestation and climate change, Mehta embarked on a mission to develop and promote sustainable solutions.

His journey began with a fascination for traditional agricultural practices and a keen observation of the depletion of fertile land. He stumbled upon the concept of biochar, a charcoal-like substance created by heating biomass in the absence of oxygen. Intrigued by its potential to improve soil health and mitigate climate change, Mehta dedicated himself to researching and developing innovative biochar production and application techniques. Under Mehta's leadership, the Progressive Biochar Society has emerged as a leading organization in the biochar sector.

The Society focuses on: Developing and promoting sustainable biochar production methods: Emphasizing the use of agricultural waste and other renewable resources as feedstock, ensuring minimal environmental impact. Research and development: Conducting rigorous scientific research to optimize biochar production processes, understand its impact on soil health, and explore new applications in agriculture, water treatment, and carbon sequestration. Capacity building and education: Training farmers, entrepreneurs, and policymakers on the benefits of biochar, promoting its adoption, and fostering a community of practitioners.

President

Dr Sai Bhaskar Reddy
Dr. Sai Bhaskar Reddy, a renowned soil scientist and environmentalist, has been instrumental in guiding the Progressive Biochar Society towards groundbreaking achievements as its President. With a deep understanding of soil ecology and a passion for sustainable agriculture, Dr. Reddy has seamlessly integrated scientific rigor with practical applications, transforming the Society into a leading force in the biochar revolution.

Prior to assuming the Presidency, Dr. Reddy conducted pioneering research on the impact of biochar on soil fertility, carbon sequestration, and crop productivity. His research findings, widely published in esteemed scientific journals, have significantly contributed to the growing body of knowledge on biochar technology. As President, Dr. Reddy has spearheaded several key initiatives: Establishing a state-of-the-art research facility: The Society now boasts a cutting-edge research facility equipped with advanced analytical instruments, enabling in-depth investigations into biochar properties, soil-biochar interactions, and the environmental impacts of biochar application.

Developing farmer-centric biochar production technologies: Recognizing the need for accessible and affordable technologies, Dr. Reddy has championed the development of low-cost, locally appropriate biochar production units, empowering farmers to produce biochar from agricultural residues.
